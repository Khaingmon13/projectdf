Fukuoka

Fukuoka is a prefecture located on the island of Kyushu, the most southern of Japan's four main islands. Fukuoka is at the center of Kyushu's politics, economy, culture and fashion and Fukuoka city, it's biggest city, is always bustling with energy. Fukuoka is also famous for being the doorway to Asia with many International flights frequenting the prefecture, with almost instant access into downtown Fukuoka from the airport. It is for this reason that Fukuoka is the most popular destination in Japan for Chinese, Korean and Taiwanese tourists to visit for quick weekend visits. Fukuoka prefecture accounts for 40% of the entire economy of Kyushu, being one of Japan's major industrial centers in Japan, with a strong business background in the automobile and steel industries. Fukuoka is also a center for gourmet and tasty street foods, with Hakata Ramen being a nationwide favorite which has since gone on to spread all over the world and become a hugely popular dish for all nationalities.

Places to Visit In Fukuoka

  • Kawachi Wisteria Garden

    The Kawachi Wisteria Garden in Kitakyushu City, Fukuoka Prefecture was selected as one of “The 31 Most Beautiful Places in Japan” by the U.S. news broadcaster CNN. It is the perfect spot to enjoy the beautiful shades of wisteria’s purple flowers. In Japanese, wisteria is called fuji, and the garden is also known as Kawachi Fuji Garden (no relation to the mountain). The garden hosts an annual Wisteria Festival from mid-April to mid-May, which is when the blooming is at its peak. Their giant 8 acre (3,305 ㎡)wisteria trellis and 360 ft. long (110 m) wisteria tunnel is truly stunning. During the festival season, there are shuttle buses from Yahata station. In autumn, from mid-November to early December, the park is also a great place to see the beautiful autumnal leaves.

    Recommended Season April･May･November

  • Sakurai Futamigaura

    Sakurai Futamigaura is a scenic spot on the coast in the west of Fukuoka Prefecture. It is famous for the 'couple rock', two rocks connected by a sacred rope called shimenawa. The union of the two rocks is meant to symbolize marriage. Sakurai Futamigaura is a popular spot for picture taking and is considered one of the top spots in Kyushu to watch the sunset. The scene of the setting sun casting its red light on the rocks is truly romantic.

    Recommended Season July･August

  • Hyugami Gorge

    Hyugami Gorge in Fukuoka Prefecture spreads out behind the Hyugami Dam for approximately 3.7mi (6 km). High hills, sturdy rocks, and a red bridge create a picturesque scene in the autumn. The ravine includes several camping grounds and trekking routes, making it ideal for bird watching or fishing. A 10m-high rock wall has been a famous site for Zen ascetic practice for about 700 years.

    Recommended Season April･August･September･October

  • Nanzoin Temple

    Nanzoin Temple is a Buddhist temple in Fukuoka Prefecture famous for its huge reclining Buddha statue. At 135ft (41 meters) in lengths, 36ft (11 meters) in height and weighing 300 tons, the reclining Buddha at Nanzoin Temple is said to be the world’s biggest bronze statue. The different poses of Buddha statues have different meanings, and the reclining posture depicts Buddha at the moment of death, or entrance into nirvana. Nanzoin Temple is the first stop of the jorney on the 88 on the Sasaguri pilgrimage route.

    Recommended Season January･March･April･May･June･July･August･September･October･November
