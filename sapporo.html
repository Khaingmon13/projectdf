Sapporo

Sapporo city is the main hub of Japan’s northern island, Hokkaido. It is the fifth-largest city in Japan in terms of population, and its population makes up for 30% of Hokkaido as a whole. Sapporo station is directly connected to Chitose Airport and offers many facilities, including a 1900m long shopping arcade. Sapporo also has abundant nature and was chosen as the first city in Asia to host the winter Olympics, in 1972. It is famous for its fresh seafood and other produce as well as for the snow festival, its large clock, and Mt. Okura. Otaru is another must-see city of Hokkaido, with it's rich and interesting history as a port city famous for its fishing and trade industry, located only half an hour away from Sapporo. Particular spots are the Otaru canal, still lined with old warehouses and with a particularly European atmosphere to it's architecture.

Places to Visit In Sapporo

  • Otaru Sushi Street

    The small city of Otaru in Hokkaido is famous for it's mouth watering sushi. Despite it's small population the city has over 130 sushi shops, offering sushi that will literally melt in your mouth it's so good. Most famous is the Sushi Street, which is a stretch of different sushi shops, small and large, and all with something special to offer to your taste buds! The city of Otaru takes pride in it's sushi, and if you want to try some of the best on whats on offer, Sushi Street is a must-go!

    Recommended Season January･February･March･April･May･June･July･August･September･October･November･December

  • Mt. Tengu

    Mt. Tengu (Tenguyama) is a mountain located in Otaru City, Hokkaido. From the mountain's top, you can see both the city and the sea of Japan, prefferably while enjoying the beautiful night views that the mountain is known to offer. Since the stars seen from the mountaintop are said to be like jewels, the mountain's peak is called one of the “Three Greatest Night Views in Hokkaido.” Also, as a ski area where the harbor can be seen in the winter, it is also a popular destination with many skiers. There is also a menagerie of playground equipment like slides, so both adults and children can enjoy it the area.

    Recommended Season January･March･April･May･July･August･September･October･November･December

  • Nikka Whiskey Yoichi Distillery

    Nikka Whiskey Yoichi Distillery of Hokkaido prefecture has managed to distinguish itself as a top of the line producer of whiskey by clinging to its rich history and production process. Being the father of Japanese whiskey, the company's founder studied the process in Scotland for several years before bringing it back to Japan and setting up a distillery. In Hokkaido, with its rich wheat fields and brisker weather, is where he set up his own distillery which remains to this day. By continuing to use traditional Scottish techniques, like the use of coal fired pot stills, oak barrels, and old copper distilleries, the Nikka brand manages to produce World-Whiskey-Award winning blended malts.

    Recommended Season January･February･March･April･May･June･July･August･September･October･November･December

  • Ao no Dokutsu Blue Cave

    The Ao no Dokutsu Blue Cave on the Shakotan peninsula in Hokkaido is a scenic spot that you can only reach by boat. The shape of the cave and the sunlight coming in at a particular angle makes the water appear in a beautiful blue tone. There are multiple cruise companies that offer boat trips to the cave between May and September as well as kayak tours for those who want to get more active. Great for a day trip from Sapporo or Otaru when the weather is good.

    Recommended Season May･June･July･August･September
